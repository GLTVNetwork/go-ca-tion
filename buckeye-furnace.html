Buckeye Furnace

A step back into Ohio's iron-making past and another memorable stop along our GO-CA-TION journey.

About This Stop

We found Buckeye Furnace while exploring southern Ohio's back roads. It wasn't a place we had planned to spend much time, but after walking the grounds it quickly became one of those stops that reminds you how much history is tucked away in small towns across Ohio.

Buckeye Furnace was originally built in 1852 and operated until 1894 as part of Ohio's Hanging Rock Iron Region. During the 1800s, furnaces like this produced charcoal iron that helped fuel Ohio's growing industries and supported the Union during the Civil War. Today the reconstructed furnace, museum, and surrounding buildings preserve an important chapter of Ohio's industrial history. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

One of the things we enjoy most about GO-CA-TION is discovering places like this. They may not be crowded tourist attractions, but they tell the story of Ohio one stop at a time.
